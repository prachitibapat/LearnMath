Subtraction

Subtraction means removing a small quantity from a larger quantity. After a number is subtracted, or removed from another, it becomes smaller.

A few illustrative questions to help you learn better:

Subtraction is the exact opposite of addition.

While in addition, the total quantity increases, in subtraction, the total quantity decreases.

While addition is represented by the + sign, subtraction is represented by the - sign

Just like addition, subtraction too follows a few rules. The signs of a number are very important. For example:

(-4) – (-3) = (-4) + (3) = -1

and

(-2) – (-8) = (-2) + 8 = 6

The – sign between the two brackets essentially unlocks the second bracket, and turns the positive number to a negative number.

The Rules Don't Apply

Do you remember all of those rules we told you about for addition? We told you about the commutative law, associative law, and addition identities. Well, none of those laws work for subtraction. You cannot reorder, shuffle, or regroup subtraction problems in the same way as addition. You need to be especially careful with subtraction. For example:

20 - 8 - 6 = 6 is not

8 - 6 - 20 = -18 (you cannot rearrange and get the same answer)

AND

(20 - 8) - 6 = 6 is not

20 - (8 - 6) = 18 (you cannot regroup and get the same answer)

Some Rules Do Apply

Some of the rules still work for subtraction. The order of operations that you use still works. Look for parentheses and work inside of those blocks first. Look at how these problems come out with very different answers if you ignore the parentheses.

For example, (20 - 8) - (6 - 4) = 12 - 2 = 10

rearranging,

20 - 8 - 6 - 4 = 2

What About Mixing Operations?

Some of your homework problems will have addition and subtraction. Can you use any of the addition laws to solve those problems or make things easier? Yes. Let's look at an example...

5 + 6 - 8 - 2 + 9 -1 = ?

So how can we work this out? Can we move things around? Yes, but only the values with addition symbols.

5 + 6 - 8 - 2 + 9 -1 = 9 and solving further...

5 + 6 + 9 - 8 - 2 - 1 = 9
